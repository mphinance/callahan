Take this one (faster, tolled)

The route your mom meant
  • I-94 SE through Milwaukee toward Chicago
  • Chicago Skyway (~$8 car)
  • Indiana Toll Road full length, ~157 mi (~$13-14 cash / ~$11 E-ZPass)
  • Ohio Turnpike west gate to the Sandusky exit (~$3-4)
  • US-250 / US-6 to the KOA on Cleveland Rd
~420 mi, ~6 hrs non-stop, plus the 1-hour time-zone jump. Tolls roughly $25-35 cash each way.

The free alternative

Skip if you're chasing the shorter clock
  • Loops up and around through Michigan to dodge the Chicago tolls
  • Longer in both miles and time, and you still hit Chicago traffic gravity
  • Only worth it if a toll-free transponder situation makes it a wash
You already said you're taking the toll route, so this is just here for the record.

Smart Money + Timing

  • Get an E-ZPass / I-PASS transponder if either car has one, it's cheaper than cash on the Indiana Toll Road and saves the booth stop.
  • Gas up before the Skyway, Illinois/Indiana toll-corridor prices run high.
  • Eastern time: leaving ~1pm Central puts you near Sandusky around 8pm Eastern with one good meal stop. Deluxe cabin check-in, so no tent setup in the dark.
  • Saturday home: leave the KOA in the morning to beat the 2pm storm chance and you're back in Colgate for a normal evening.
