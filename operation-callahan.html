Stuff You Probably Didn't Think Of

  • Stake down for Thursday. 37 mph gusts will walk a canopy across the campground. Extra stakes, and leave the RV awning in until Friday.
  • Cedar Point ride lockers are credit-only. No coins, no debit. Have a real card on the lead adult.
  • Pre-buy/scan tickets and Fast Lane at the campsite Thursday night on hotel wifi, not in a dead-zone line at the gate.
  • Photo with the birthday button at the front sign first thing, before everyone's sweaty and melting down.
  • A "lost kid" plan. Snap a photo of Kilian each morning in what he's wearing, write your phone # on a card in his pocket, pick a meetup landmark.
  • Bottled water for the KOA tap. Reviews ding the water quality. Bring drinking water.
  • Ziploc the phones before Snake River Falls and Thunder Canyon. You will get soaked, that's the point.
  • One slow morning. Two days is a lot of road + park for an 8-year-old. Build in a campsite jumping-pillow morning so it stays fun.

The KOA (Sandusky / Bayshore)

  • Closest campground to Cedar Point, about a mile east of Cedar Point Drive on US-6 / Cleveland Rd. Short hop to the gate.
  • On-site for the birthday energy: jumping pillow, gem mine, pedal carts, snack bar, family movie night, catch-and-release fishing. Built-in backup fun if anyone's coastered-out.
  • Pet-friendly with a dog park, if a dog is in the picture.
  • They sell firewood and propane on-site, so a forgotten bundle isn't a crisis.
  • Confirm check-in time and the reservation under the right name before you roll Thursday.
